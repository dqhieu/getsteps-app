# How Many Steps in 5 km?

**5 km is approximately 6,300-7,000 steps** for most adults walking at a moderate pace. The exact number depends on your height and stride length — taller walkers cover more ground per step, while shorter walkers need more steps to clear the same distance.

If you just finished a 5 km walk and want to know what it added to your daily step count, or you are converting a step total back into kilometers, this guide gives you the numbers — plus tables for time, calories, and pace.

## How Many Steps Are in 5 Kilometers?

5 kilometers equals 5,000 meters or about 3.107 miles. At an average walking stride of 0.75 m (about 2.5 ft), that works out to roughly **6,667 steps** for a person of average height.

Quick reference for walking 5 km at a moderate pace:

- **Short adult (5'2" / 157 cm):** ~7,500 steps
- **Average adult (5'7" / 170 cm):** ~6,700 steps
- **Tall adult (6'1" / 185 cm):** ~6,100 steps

The most common range you will see for 5 km is **6,300 to 7,000 steps** for moderate walking. Brisk walkers land closer to 6,000-6,500 because faster walking lengthens stride. ## 5 km vs 5K Run: How Steps Differ

A 5K race covers the same distance as 5 km — they are identical at 3.107 miles. The difference is how you cover it. Running shifts your stride from about 0.75 m to 1.0-1.2 m, so the same 5 kilometers takes fewer total steps.

Side-by-side comparison for an average-height adult:

| Activity | Stride | Steps in 5 km | Time |
|----------|--------|---------------|------|
| Moderate walk | 0.75 m | ~6,700 | 62 min |
| Brisk walk | 0.80 m | ~6,250 | 54 min |
| Light jog | 0.95 m | ~5,300 | 38 min |
| Moderate run | 1.10 m | ~4,550 | 30 min |
| Fast run | 1.25 m | ~4,000 | 22 min |

Running 5 km cuts roughly 1,500-2,500 steps off the walking count, but each step lands harder and burns more energy. For event-focused 5K training tips, pacing strategies, and walk-run plans, read our dedicated guide on [how many steps in a 5K](/blog/how-many-steps-in-a-5k).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is 5 km equal to 6,000 steps?

For most adults, 6,000 steps covers slightly less than 5 km — closer to **4.3 to 4.8 km**. The actual 5 km step count is **6,300-7,000 steps** for walking. Tall walkers may hit 5 km in under 6,200 steps, while shorter walkers may need 7,500+.

### How many steps is a 5 km walk for a woman?

The average woman (5'4" / 163 cm) takes roughly **7,000-7,200 steps** to walk 5 km at a moderate pace. Taller women take fewer steps and shorter women take more — height matters more than gender itself.

### How many steps is a 5 km walk for a man?

The average man (5'9" / 175 cm) takes about **6,400-6,600 steps** to walk 5 km. Men's average step counts are lower than women's mainly because of average height differences.

### How many steps in 5 km running?

Running 5 km typically takes **4,000-5,000 steps**, depending on your height and pace. Faster runners with longer strides land closer to 4,000, while shorter or slower runners take closer to 5,000.

### Is walking 5 km a day enough exercise?

Yes — walking 5 km a day puts you well within the moderate-activity zone recommended by most public health guidelines. It adds **6,300-7,000 steps** to your daily total, burns 200-400 calories, and takes under an hour at a moderate pace. Combined with normal daily movement, it gets most people close to a 10,000-step goal.
