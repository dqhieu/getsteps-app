# Calories Burned Per Step: Full Breakdown by Weight, Pace & Terrain

**How many calories are burned per step?** The average adult burns roughly **0.03 to 0.06 calories per step**, depending on body weight, walking pace, and the surface underfoot. For a 160 lb person walking at a moderate pace on flat ground, one step burns approximately 0.04 calories. That translates to about 40 calories per 1,000 steps and 400 calories per 10,000 steps.

Knowing the exact calories burned per step lets you reverse-engineer any fitness goal — whether you want to [burn 100 calories](/blog/how-many-steps-to-burn-100-calories), [burn 500 calories](/blog/how-many-steps-burn-500-calories), or simply understand what your daily step count is actually doing for your body. This guide gives you the tables, the formula, and the practical context to put that number to work.

## The Formula Behind Calories Per Step

Every calorie-per-step estimate traces back to MET values — the Metabolic Equivalent of Task. Here is the formula:

**Calories per step = MET x weight (kg) x 0.000175 x time per step (minutes)**

Breaking that down:

- **MET** represents exercise intensity. A slow walk has a MET of about 2.5; brisk walking sits around 4.3.
- **Weight in kg** accounts for the energy cost of moving your body mass.
- **0.000175** is the standard conversion factor (calories per kg per minute per MET unit, divided to the per-step level).
- **Time per step** depends on cadence. At 100 steps per minute (moderate pace), each step takes 0.01 minutes. At 130 steps per minute (brisk), each step takes about 0.0077 minutes.

For a quick mental shortcut: **multiply your weight in pounds by 0.00025** to estimate calories burned per step at a moderate pace. A 160 lb person gets 0.04 calories per step; a 200 lb person gets 0.05.

Understanding the MET component is key — see our [MET Values for Walking](/blog/met-values-walking-chart) chart for a complete reference across different walking speeds and inclines.

## How Terrain Changes Calories Burned Per Step

Walking on flat pavement is the baseline. Real-world walking rarely stays flat. Here is how terrain shifts calories per step for a 160 lb person walking at a moderate pace:

| Terrain | Calories Per Step | vs. Flat Ground |
|---|---|---|
| Flat pavement | 0.040 cal | Baseline |
| Grass or packed dirt | 0.042 cal | +5% |
| Sand (firm, wet) | 0.046 cal | +15% |
| Uphill (5% grade) | 0.050 cal | +25% |
| Uphill (10% grade) | 0.060 cal | +50% |
| Loose sand or snow | 0.056 cal | +40% |
| Hiking trail (mixed) | 0.052 cal | +30% |

Uneven and soft surfaces force your stabilizer muscles to work harder with every step. That is why a beach walk or a hiking trail burns noticeably more calories per step than walking the same distance on a sidewalk — even if your speed feels identical.

## How to Increase Calories Burned Per Step

If you want to burn more calories without adding more steps to your day, focus on intensity per step rather than volume:

**1. Walk faster.** Increasing your pace from 3.0 mph to 3.5 mph boosts calories burned per step by about 20%. A [walking workout plan](/blog/walking-workout-plan-beginners) can help you build speed gradually.

**2. Add incline.** Walking uphill at a 5% grade increases your per-step calorie burn by roughly 25%. Use hills in your neighborhood or set your treadmill to an incline.

**3. Carry weight.** A weighted vest or backpack (5–10% of body weight) raises the energy cost of each step without changing your route.

**4. Walk on softer surfaces.** Grass, trails, and sand demand more muscular effort per step than concrete. Even switching from pavement to a park path makes a measurable difference.

**5. Shorten your stride slightly.** A shorter stride at the same speed means higher cadence and more muscle activation per minute. Your calories per step may dip slightly, but your calories per minute increase.

## Calories Per Step: Walking vs. Running

Walking and running burn different calories per step because the biomechanics differ:

| Activity | Calories Per Step (160 lbs) | Steps Per Minute |
|---|---|---|
| Slow walk (2.0 mph) | 0.033 cal | ~85 |
| Moderate walk (3.0 mph) | 0.040 cal | ~105 |
| Brisk walk (4.0 mph) | 0.048 cal | ~125 |
| Jogging (5.0 mph) | 0.070 cal | ~155 |
| Running (6.5 mph) | 0.090 cal | ~170 |

Running roughly doubles the calories burned per step compared to moderate walking. However, walking wins on sustainability — most people can walk 10,000+ steps daily but would struggle to run that volume without injury risk.

The sweet spot for many people is brisk walking at 3.5–4.0 mph. You get meaningfully higher calories per step without the joint impact of running.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many calories are burned per step on average?

The average adult burns approximately **0.03 to 0.05 calories per step** at a moderate walking pace. The exact number depends primarily on body weight — a 140 lb person burns about 0.034 calories per step, while a 200 lb person burns closer to 0.05 calories per step. Pace, terrain, and fitness level also play a role.

### How do I calculate calories burned per step for my weight?

Use the formula: **Calories per step = MET x weight (kg) x 0.000175 x time per step (minutes)**. For a quick estimate, multiply your weight in pounds by 0.00025. Or use our [Steps to Calories Calculator](/tools/steps-to-calories-calculator) to get an instant result tailored to your profile.

### Does walking speed change calories burned each step?

Yes. Walking faster increases the MET value of the activity, which raises calories burned each step. A brisk pace (3.5–4.0 mph) burns roughly 20–35% more calories per step than a slow stroll (2.0 mph). The faster pace also means higher cadence, so your per-minute calorie burn increases even more.

### Are calories burned per step the same on a treadmill and outdoors?

On a flat treadmill with no incline, calorie burn per step is about 3–5% lower than outdoor walking because there is no wind resistance and no surface variation. Setting the treadmill to a 1% incline closely replicates the calorie cost of walking on flat ground outdoors.

### How many steps does it take to burn 100 calories?

Most adults need **2,000 to 3,000 steps** to burn 100 calories at a moderate pace. The exact count depends on weight: a 200 lb person reaches 100 calories in about 2,000 steps, while a 130 lb person needs closer to 3,000 steps. See the full breakdown in [How Many Steps to Burn 100 Calories?](/blog/how-many-steps-to-burn-100-calories).

### Does body composition affect calories per step?

Yes, though weight is the bigger factor. Two people at the same weight but different body compositions (more muscle vs. more fat) will burn slightly different calories per step. Muscle tissue has a higher metabolic rate, so a more muscular person burns marginally more calories per step — typically 5–10% more at the same weight and pace.
