# How Many Steps in 10 km?

**10 km is approximately 12,500-14,000 steps** for most adults walking at a moderate pace. Your exact number depends on your height and stride length — taller walkers cover more ground per step, so they need fewer steps to finish 10 kilometers. Shorter walkers take more steps for the same distance.

10 km is a meaningful distance: a 10K race, a long hike, or a serious daily walking goal. This guide covers exactly how many steps you'll take, how long it'll take, and how many calories you'll burn.

## How Many Steps Are in 10 Kilometers?

Quick answer for the most common scenarios:

- **Walking (moderate pace):** ~13,000-14,000 steps
- **Brisk walking:** ~12,000-13,500 steps
- **Running:** ~10,000-12,000 steps

The most-cited average is roughly **13,120 steps for 10 km** at a moderate walking pace for an adult of average height (5'7" / 170 cm). That's based on about 1,312 steps per kilometer — see our [how many steps in a kilometer](/blog/how-many-steps-in-a-kilometer) breakdown for the math.

## How Long Does It Take to Walk 10 Km?

A 10 km walk is a real time commitment — typically **1.5 to 2.5 hours** depending on your pace. This is not a quick errand walk; plan for it like a workout. Here's how the timing breaks down:

| Pace | Speed | Time for 10 km |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Slow / casual | 3.0 km/h | 3 hr 20 min |
| Easy walk | 4.0 km/h | 2 hr 30 min |
| Moderate walk | 4.8 km/h | 2 hr 5 min |
| Brisk walk | 6.0 km/h | 1 hr 40 min |
| Power walk | 7.0 km/h | 1 hr 26 min |
| Easy jog | 8.0 km/h | 1 hr 15 min |
| Run | 10.0 km/h | 1 hr |
| Fast run | 12.0 km/h | 50 min |

Most recreational walkers finish 10 km in about **2 hours** at a moderate pace. New to long walks? Plan for 2.5 hours and take rest stops. ## 10 Km vs 10K Run: Steps Compared

A 10K race is also 10 kilometers — same distance, different stride. Running a 10K takes meaningfully fewer steps than walking it, because each running stride is longer.

For the average adult (5'7" / 170 cm):

- **Walking 10 km:** ~14,120 steps, ~2 hours
- **Jogging 10K (8 km/h):** ~11,500 steps, ~75 minutes
- **Running 10K (10 km/h):** ~11,040 steps, ~60 minutes
- **Fast 10K (12 km/h):** ~10,200 steps, ~50 minutes

Runners cover 10 km in roughly **20-25% fewer steps** than walkers, but each running step burns more — a 10K run burns about 1.5x the calories of a 10 km walk. See [steps in a mile running](/blog/how-many-steps-in-a-mile-running) for imperial equivalents.

## Tips for a 10 Km Walk

10 km is far enough that prep matters. A few notes from people who do it regularly:

**Hydrate before and during.** Drink a full glass of water 30 minutes before you start, and bring at least 500 ml with you. On hot days, double that.

**Pace yourself early.** The most common mistake is starting too fast. Aim for a pace where you can still hold a conversation — if you're breathing heavily in the first kilometer, slow down.

**Build up gradually.** Don't jump straight to 10 km. Work up through [5 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-5-km), [6 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-6-km), and [8 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-8-km) over a few weeks so your feet, hips, and lower back can adapt.

**Pick the right shoes.** 14,000 steps in shoes that don't fit will leave you with blisters. Cushioned, broken-in walking or running shoes are non-negotiable.

**Fuel for it.** A 10 km walk burns 400-700 calories — eat something light beforehand (banana, toast, oatmeal) and bring a small snack if you'll be out longer than 90 minutes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many steps is 10 km exactly?

For most adults, 10 km equals **12,500 to 15,200 steps** when walking at a moderate pace. The average is around **13,000-14,000 steps**. Your exact count depends on your height.

### Is walking 10 km a day too much?

Walking 10 km daily is a substantial workout — about 2 hours and 13,000+ steps. Safe and beneficial for most healthy adults, but build up gradually if you're new to long walks. Most guidelines suggest 30-60 minutes of moderate activity daily; 10 km sits on the upper end.

### How many calories does walking 10 km burn?

A 75 kg adult walking 10 km at a moderate pace burns about **490 calories**. Heavier walkers and faster paces burn up to 700+ calories. Running the same distance burns roughly 600-900 calories.

### How long does it take to walk 10 km?

At a moderate pace (4.8 km/h), most adults finish 10 km in about **2 hours**. Brisk walkers finish in around 1 hour 40 minutes; casual walkers take 2.5 hours or more.

### Is 10 km the same as 10,000 steps?

No. 10 km is roughly **13,000-14,000 steps**. 10,000 steps actually equals about **7.4 km** — see [how many km is 10,000 steps](/blog/how-many-km-is-10000-steps) for that conversion. Different goal, different distance.
