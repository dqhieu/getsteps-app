# How Many Steps in a Kilometer?

**How many steps in a kilometer?** Most adults take between **1,250 and 1,550 steps per kilometer** when walking at a comfortable pace. The exact number depends on your height, stride length, and speed. Taller walkers cover more ground per step, so they need fewer steps to complete a kilometer. Shorter walkers take more steps to cover the same distance.

Whether you track your walks in metric or need to convert steps to km for a race goal, this guide gives you precise numbers — including a height-based steps-per-km chart, a km-to-steps quick reference table, and the differences between walking and running.

## Steps in a KM: Quick Answer

Here's the short version:

- **Walking (moderate pace):** ~1,300–1,400 steps per km
- **Brisk walking:** ~1,200–1,350 steps per km
- **Running:** ~1,000–1,200 steps per km

The most-cited average is roughly **1,312 steps per kilometer** for an adult of average height (5'7" / 170 cm) walking at a moderate pace. But your personal number could differ by 200+ steps depending on your body and speed.

## Walking vs. Running: How Many Steps in a Kilometer?

Walking and running produce very different step counts for the same kilometer. When you run, your stride lengthens significantly — sometimes by 40–60% compared to walking. Here's how the numbers compare for someone of average height (5'7" / 170 cm):

| Activity | Steps Per KM | Time Per KM |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Casual walk (3.0 km/h) | ~1,480 | ~20 min |
| Moderate walk (4.8 km/h) | ~1,412 | ~12.5 min |
| Brisk walk (6.0 km/h) | ~1,298 | ~10 min |
| Jogging (8.0 km/h) | ~1,150 | ~7.5 min |
| Running (10.0 km/h) | ~1,104 | ~6 min |
| Fast running (12.0 km/h) | ~1,020 | ~5 min |

The key insight: **running a kilometer takes roughly 20–25% fewer steps than walking it**, but each step covers more distance and burns more energy per step. For a deeper dive on running step counts, see our guide on [how many steps in a mile running](/blog/how-many-steps-in-a-mile-running).

Running also burns more [calories per step](/blog/calories-burned-per-step) due to higher [MET values](/blog/met-values-walking-chart) — about 1.5 to 2 times more than walking at the same distance.

## What Affects Your Steps Per Kilometer?

Several factors determine how many steps you take in a km beyond just height:

### 1. Stride Length

Stride length is the distance between successive footfalls of the same foot. It's determined primarily by your leg length (and thus height) but also by flexibility, hip mobility, and walking habits. A longer stride means fewer steps per kilometer.

### 2. Walking Speed

As you speed up, your stride naturally lengthens. A casual stroll produces about 8–12% more steps per km than a brisk walk. This is why the "brisk walk" column in the chart above shows lower step counts.

### 3. Terrain

Flat pavement allows your longest, most efficient stride. Hills, trails, sand, and uneven ground shorten your stride, increasing your step count per kilometer. Expect 5–15% more steps per km on hilly terrain compared to flat ground.

### 4. Age and Fitness

Stride length tends to decrease with age. Older adults may take 10–15% more steps per kilometer than younger adults of the same height. Regular walking and flexibility exercises can help maintain stride length over time.

### 5. Footwear

Supportive, well-fitted shoes allow a natural, full stride. Heavy boots or worn-out shoes can shorten your stride and increase your step count per kilometer.

## How to Find Your Personal Steps Per KM

The charts above are estimates. For your exact number, try one of these methods:

**Method 1 — Walk a measured kilometer.** Many parks have marked paths. Reset your step counter, walk exactly 1 km at your normal pace, and note the count.

**Method 2 — Measure your stride.** Walk 20 steps on flat ground, measure the total distance, and divide by 20. Then divide 1,000 meters by your stride length in meters to get your steps per km.

## FAQ: Steps in a Kilometer

### How many steps is 1 km?

For most adults, **1 km equals about 1,250 to 1,550 steps** when walking. The average is roughly 1,300–1,400 steps per kilometer. Your exact number depends on your height and walking speed — taller and faster walkers take fewer steps.

### How do I convert km to steps?

Multiply the distance in kilometers by your steps per km. For example, if you take 1,400 steps per km: 5 km x 1,400 = 7,000 steps. For an instant conversion, use our [Step Distance Calculator](/tools/step-distance-calculator).

### How do I convert steps to km?

Divide your total steps by your steps per km. For example: 10,000 steps / 1,400 steps per km = 7.14 km. You can also use the [Step Distance Calculator](/tools/step-distance-calculator) for automatic conversions.

### Is 1,000 steps equal to 1 km?

No. 1,000 steps is less than 1 km for most people. At an average of 1,300–1,400 steps per km, 1,000 steps covers about **0.7 to 0.77 km** (roughly 700–770 meters).

### How many steps in a kilometer for a woman?

The average woman (5'4" / 163 cm) takes about **1,400 to 1,500 steps per kilometer** when walking at a moderate pace. Taller women take fewer steps, shorter women take more.

### How many steps in a kilometer for a man?

The average man (5'9" / 175 cm) takes about **1,300 to 1,400 steps per kilometer** at a moderate pace. The difference between men and women is primarily due to average height differences, not gender itself.

### How many steps in a km when running?

Running a kilometer typically takes **1,000 to 1,200 steps**, depending on your height and speed. Faster runners have longer strides and take fewer steps per km.

### How many kilometers is 10,000 steps?

At an average of 1,350 steps per km, 10,000 steps equals approximately **7.4 km** (about 4.6 miles). Shorter people may cover closer to 6.5 km, while taller people may cover 8+ km.
