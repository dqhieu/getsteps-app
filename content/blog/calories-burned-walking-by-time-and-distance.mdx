# Calories Burned Walking: 30 Minutes, 1 Hour, and Beyond

How many calories does walking actually burn? The short answer: a 155-pound person burns roughly **150 calories in 30 minutes** and **300 calories in 1 hour** of moderate walking. But the exact number depends on your weight, speed, and terrain.

Here's a complete breakdown with reference tables so you can find your number instantly—plus a [free walking calories calculator](/tools/walking-calories-calculator) for a personalized estimate.

## Calories Burned Walking 30 Minutes

Walking for 30 minutes is the most popular workout duration. Here's what you burn at a moderate pace (3.5 mph / 5.6 km/h):

| Body Weight | Calories Burned (30 min) |
|-------------|-------------------------|
| 120 lbs (54 kg) | 107 |
| 140 lbs (64 kg) | 125 |
| 155 lbs (70 kg) | 150 |
| 180 lbs (82 kg) | 167 |
| 200 lbs (91 kg) | 186 |
| 220 lbs (100 kg) | 204 |

These numbers use a MET value of 3.5 for moderate-pace walking. Walking faster (brisk pace at 4.0 mph) bumps the MET to 4.3, increasing calorie burn by about 20%.

## How Walking Calorie Burn Is Calculated

The formula behind all calorie calculations uses **MET values** (Metabolic Equivalent of Task):

```
Calories = MET × weight (kg) × time (hours)
```

Here are the MET values for different walking speeds:

| Walking Speed | MET Value |
|--------------|-----------|
| Slow (2.0 mph / 3.2 km/h) | 2.5 |
| Moderate (3.0 mph / 5.0 km/h) | 3.5 |
| Brisk (3.5 mph / 5.6 km/h) | 4.3 |
| Fast (4.0 mph / 6.4 km/h) | 5.0 |
| Very Fast (4.5 mph / 7.2 km/h) | 5.7 |

**Example:** A 155-pound (70 kg) person walking briskly for 1 hour:
- 4.3 MET × 70 kg × 1 hour = **301 calories**

## How to Burn More Calories Walking

If you want to maximize calorie burn without switching to running:

1. **Walk faster** — Increasing from 3.0 to 4.0 mph boosts burn by 40%+
2. **Add inclines** — Walking uphill increases MET to 6.0–8.0
3. **Use walking poles** — Nordic walking burns 15-20% more calories
4. **Carry a weighted vest** — Even 10 lbs adds meaningful resistance
5. **Walk after meals** — Post-meal walks improve glucose metabolism

## FAQ

### How many calories does walking 1 mile burn?

A 155-pound person burns about **80-100 calories per mile** at a moderate pace. Heavier people burn more, lighter people burn less. The speed doesn't change per-mile calorie burn significantly—it mainly affects how many miles you cover per hour.

### Is walking 10,000 steps enough to lose weight?

It depends on your diet. Walking 10,000 steps burns 300-500 calories depending on your weight. Combined with a slight calorie deficit in your diet, this can lead to 0.5-1 pound of weight loss per week. ### Does walking on a treadmill burn the same calories?

Treadmill walking burns slightly fewer calories than outdoor walking because there's no wind resistance and the belt assists your stride. Setting the incline to 1% roughly compensates for this difference.

### How accurate are fitness trackers at counting walking calories?
