## How Pedometer Apps Work on iPhone

Every iPhone has a motion coprocessor (M-series chip) that tracks movement in the background. Pedometer apps read this data through Apple's HealthKit framework.

This means:

- **All HealthKit apps report the same step count** — accuracy doesn't vary between apps
- **Steps are tracked even when the app isn't open** — the chip runs constantly
- **Battery impact is minimal** — the motion coprocessor is low-power

The difference between pedometer apps isn't accuracy — it's how they present your data and what extra features they offer.

## What to Look For in a Pedometer App

### Widgets and Glanceability

The best pedometer apps let you see your steps without opening the app. Look for home screen widgets, lock screen widgets, and Apple Watch complications.

### Goal Tracking and Streaks

A daily step goal gives you something to aim for. Streak tracking adds motivation — you won't want to break a 30-day streak.

### Workout Logging

Basic pedometers only count steps. Better apps also log workouts with GPS, distance, pace, and calories.

### Data Ownership

Your fitness data should be yours. Look for apps that sync with Apple Health and offer export options like GPX files.

## Common Questions About Pedometer Apps

### Are pedometer apps accurate on iPhone?

Yes. All pedometer apps that use HealthKit read from the same motion sensors. Step counts are identical across apps — the difference is in features, not accuracy.

### Do pedometer apps drain battery?

No. The iPhone's motion coprocessor tracks steps using very little power. A pedometer app that reads from HealthKit adds negligible battery drain.

### Can a pedometer app count steps without internet?

Yes. Step counting happens on-device using the motion coprocessor. No internet connection is required.

### What's the difference between a pedometer and a fitness tracker?

A pedometer counts steps. A fitness tracker does that plus tracks workouts, heart rate, calories, and more. Steps: Workout & Pedometer does both — it's a pedometer with full fitness tracking built in.

### How many steps should I walk per day?
