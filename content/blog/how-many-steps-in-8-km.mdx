# How Many Steps in 8 km?

**8 km is approximately 10,000-11,200 steps** for most adults — almost exactly the classic 10,000-step daily target. Walking 8 kilometers in a single session (or spread across the day) is one of the simplest ways to hit the famous wellness benchmark while clocking real cardiovascular distance.

Your exact step count depends on your height, stride length, and pace. Taller walkers cover more ground per step and finish 8 km in fewer steps. Shorter walkers — or anyone walking on hills or rough terrain — will see counts closer to 12,000+.

## How Many Steps Are in 8 Kilometers?

For a quick answer:

- **Average adult (5'7" / 170 cm):** ~11,300 steps
- **Shorter adult (5'2" / 157 cm):** ~12,100 steps
- **Taller adult (6'1" / 185 cm):** ~10,400 steps
- **Running 8 km:** ~8,200-9,600 steps

The most-cited average is roughly **1,400 steps per kilometer** at a moderate walking pace. Multiply by 8 and you land near 11,200 — right on top of the 10,000-step goal that fitness trackers have promoted for decades.

## How Long Does It Take to Walk 8 km?

Walking 8 kilometers is a meaningful chunk of time — most adults will spend between **80 and 120 minutes** on the road, depending on pace.

| Pace | Speed | Time for 8 km |
|------|-------|---------------|
| Casual stroll | 3.0 km/h | ~2 hr 40 min |
| Easy walk | 4.0 km/h | ~2 hr 0 min |
| Moderate walk | 4.8 km/h | ~1 hr 40 min |
| Brisk walk | 5.5 km/h | ~1 hr 27 min |
| Power walk | 6.5 km/h | ~1 hr 14 min |
| Light jog | 8.0 km/h | ~1 hour |
| Easy run | 10.0 km/h | ~48 min |

A standard moderate pace lands you at roughly 100 minutes — practical to break into a 50 minute morning walk and a 50 minute evening walk if you do not want to do it all at once.

## 8 km a Day for Weight Loss

Walking 8 km a day overlaps almost perfectly with the popular **10,000 steps a day** target — the two goals are essentially equivalent for an average-height adult. That makes 8 km a smart, distance-based way to commit to the same habit.

Here is what 8 km a day produces over time at a moderate pace:

- **Per day:** ~10,000-11,200 steps, ~350-450 kcal
- **Per week:** ~70,000-78,400 steps, ~2,450-3,150 kcal
- **Per month:** ~300,000-336,000 steps, ~10,500-13,500 kcal
- **Per year:** ~3.65-4.1 million steps, ~125,000-160,000 kcal

A pound of fat is roughly 3,500 kcal, so consistent daily 8 km walks (without overeating to compensate) can plausibly drive **3-4 pounds of fat loss per month** for many adults. Pair walking with a modest calorie deficit and the impact compounds.

The benefits go beyond weight loss. Daily walking is linked to lower blood pressure, better mood, improved sleep, and reduced risk of chronic disease. See our deeper dive on the [benefits of 10,000 steps a day](/blog/10000-steps-a-day-benefits) — most apply to the 8 km equivalent.

If 8 km feels like too much in one go, ramp up gradually. Start with [2 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-2-km) or [3 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-3-km) walks, build to [5 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-5-km) and [6 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-6-km), then progress to 8 km — and eventually [10 km](/blog/how-many-steps-in-10-km) if you want a real fitness challenge.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many steps is 8 km?

For most adults, **8 km equals 10,000-11,200 steps** when walking at a moderate pace. Shorter people may take closer to 12,000 steps, taller people closer to 10,400. Running 8 km typically takes 8,200-9,600 steps.

### Is walking 8 km a day good?

Yes — 8 km a day is an excellent fitness routine for most adults. It hits the classic 10,000-step target, burns 300-600 calories, and supports cardiovascular health, weight management, and mental wellbeing. See our [10,000 steps a day benefits guide](/blog/10000-steps-a-day-benefits) for the full evidence base.

### How long does it take to walk 8 km?

At a moderate pace of 4.8 km/h (3 mph), 8 km takes about **100 minutes** — roughly 1 hour 40 minutes. A brisk pace cuts that to about 87 minutes, and a power-walk pace gets you there in about 74 minutes.

### How many calories does walking 8 km burn?

Walking 8 km burns about **300-600 calories** for most adults. A 68 kg (150 lb) person burns ~350 kcal at a moderate pace; a 95 kg (210 lb) person burns ~490 kcal at the same pace. Faster paces and hills increase the total.

### Is 8 km the same as 10,000 steps?

For an average-height adult, **yes — 8 km is essentially equivalent to 10,000 steps**. The exact match depends on your height: shorter walkers reach 10,000 steps at roughly 7 km, while taller walkers may need 8.5+ km. See our [10,000 steps to km guide](/blog/how-many-km-is-10000-steps) for the precise conversion.

### How many steps in 5 miles?

8 km is just under 5 miles (4.97 mi). Most adults take **10,000-11,200 steps to walk 5 miles**, which matches the 8 km step range almost exactly.
